Ingredients:


For the cake:

1 and 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
1 and 1/2 cups granulated sugar
3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
1 and 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
1 teaspoon baking soda
1/2 teaspoon salt
3/4 cup buttermilk
1/2 cup vegetable oil
2 large eggs
2 teaspoons vanilla extract
3/4 cup boiling water


For the raspberry filling:

1 cup fresh or frozen raspberries
1/4 cup granulated sugar
1 tablespoon cornstarch
1 tablespoon lemon juice
Pinch of salt


For the ganache:

1 cup semisweet chocolate chips
1/2 cup heavy cream
2 tablespoons unsalted butter
Pinch of salt
Instructions:

Preheat your oven to 350Â°F. Grease and flour two 9-inch cake pans, or line them with parchment paper.

In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. 
Add the buttermilk, vegetable oil, eggs, and vanilla extract, and beat until well combined. 
Gradually stir in the boiling water, mixing until the batter is smooth and thin.

Divide the batter evenly between the prepared cake pans, and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick 
inserted in the center of the cakes comes out clean.

While the cakes are baking, prepare the raspberry filling. In a small saucepan, combine the raspberries, 
sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and salt. Cook over medium heat, stirring frequently, until the mixture thickens 
and the raspberries break down, about 5-7 minutes. Remove from heat and let cool.

Once the cakes are baked, let them cool in the pans for 10 minutes, then remove them from the pans and 
let them cool completely on wire racks.

To make the ganache, place the chocolate chips in a heatproof bowl. In a small saucepan, heat the heavy cream 
and butter over medium heat until the butter is melted and the cream is hot. Pour the cream mixture over 
the chocolate chips and let sit for a minute, then whisk until smooth. Stir in the salt.

To assemble the cake, place one cake layer on a cake plate or stand. Spread the raspberry filling evenly 
over the top. Place the second cake layer on top of the filling, and pour the ganache over the cake, 
spreading it to the edges and allowing it to drip down the sides. Let the cake sit for 10-15 minutes to allow the ganache to set.

Slice and serve the cake, and let the mysterious and alluring flavors of chocolate and raspberry 
tantalize your taste buds and intrigue your senses.
